Zetech Foundation History

Founder

Zetech University was founded by Engineer Ken Mbiuki in 1999, but its roots trace back to 1997 when he began offering
IT training in his University of Nairobi hostel room. Initially operating as Zenith Technologies, he later rebranded to
Zetech College, a combination of "Zenith" and "Technology". The institution grew from a small IT training venture to a fully-fledged
university, receiving its charter in 2022.

Key aspects of the founder's history and background:

Early Beginnings:


Ken Mbiuki, then a Mechanical Engineering student at the University of Nairobi,
started offering IT training to fellow students in his hostel room in 1997.

Growth and Expansion:


The demand for IT training grew, leading to the establishment of Zetech College in 1999
and subsequent expansion to larger premises and a second campus.

Strategic Partnerships:


Zetech partnered with Jomo Kenyatta University of Agriculture and Technology to allow students to earn degrees.

Conversion to University:


After years of growth and development, Zetech College was converted into Zetech University in 2018 following approval
from the Ministry of Education.

Focus on Practical Training:


Zetech University is known for its practical training approach and strong industry links, preparing students for the job market.

Leadership:


Ken Mbiuki is the Chairman of the Board of Trustees of Zetech University.
